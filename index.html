The bigger question

You're on the wrong platform.

Every problem in the site read above can be fixed on your current setup. But most of them wouldn't happen at all on the platform you should actually be on.

Here's the honest tradeoff.

What you have now: WordPress

  • WordPress is designed for blogs and content sites. Someone added a shopping cart on top of it later.
  • Because it's built out of many separate tools stacked together, the tools can fight each other. That's why your shop page broke: two different page builders got in each other's way and nobody noticed.
  • You (or someone on your team) has to manage security updates, credit card safety, and tool conflicts. If any of that gets missed, the site can break or go offline.
  • Setting up email automations, product search results, and analytics requires manually installing and configuring many small tools that can break with updates.

Where a brand at your tier should be: Shopify

  • Shopify is designed for online stores from day one. It's what Van Leeuwen, Halo Top, Salt & Straw, and most premium ice cream brands run on.
  • Shopify handles all the behind-the-scenes work automatically: security, credit card safety, updates, backups. Nothing to manage on your end.
  • Your email tool connects with one click. Google recognizes your products automatically. Your shop page cannot break the way yours currently is because there are no fighting tools underneath.
  • Editorial control is just as good as WordPress. You can still publish blog posts, edit pages, add photos, all yourself.
  • The problems in the site read cannot happen on Shopify.

My recommendation: move Creamalicious to Shopify. Sixty days, one flat fee, everything delivered and handed off to your team at the end. Better foundation than fixing what's there piece by piece.

If you'd rather stay where you are, the plan adapts. Same sixty days, same price, different scope. That decision is the first thing we'd talk through on a call.

Weeks 1-2

Setup.

Set up a private practice version of the new Shopify store

All the building happens on a Shopify practice store nobody can see except us. Your current live website keeps running exactly as it is. Nothing your customers see changes until launch day. Think of it as building the new store next door to the one you're operating out of.

Set up the automated email tool and connect it to the new store

Klaviyo is the email tool most premium DTC brands use. It sends welcome emails to new signups, order confirmations after purchases, reminders about items in cart, and follow-ups after delivery. All automatic. Connects to your new store in about two clicks. Also gets the sending set up so emails come from hello@yourdomain and don't land in spam.

Install the tools that count who visits your site

Right now your site is a black box. No way to know how many people visit, where they came from, what they clicked, what they bought. I install the tools (Google Analytics, Meta's tracking pixel) that count everything so every future decision is based on real numbers, and so you can retarget visitors with Instagram ads later if you want to.

Before we move anything, I write down where things stand today: email list size (if any), how many people are buying, where traffic comes from. That becomes the "before" picture. In Month 3, we compare directly to prove the new site moved the numbers in the right direction.

Every flavor's page reviewed: description, image, price, ingredients, category. Cleanup opportunities flagged (like the products still labeled "COMING SOON JUNE 2026" past that date). Then every other page on the current site cataloged so nothing gets lost when we switch.
